Answer The Call

Volunteer your time. Serve your community. No experience required — we'll train you.

Why Join GFR?

Make a Real Difference

Becoming a volunteer with Gretna Fire & Rescue is about heart, dedication, and a commitment to making a difference. Whether you're answering a fire call, providing emergency medical care, or supporting behind the scenes, every role is vital to protecting and serving our community.

Our members are your neighbors — people who live and work right here in Gretna. We provide full training at no cost, and we welcome people of all backgrounds and experience levels. If you have the drive to serve, we have a place for you.

Ready to Apply?

Submit your membership application online and a department representative will contact you with next steps. The process is straightforward and we're here to help every step of the way.

Questions? Email us at recruitment@gretnafirerescue.com

Open Roles

Find Your Role

🔥 Fire

Volunteer Firefighters

Respond to diverse incidents as part of a disciplined team trained to handle any emergency.

  • Fire suppression and rescue operations
  • Hazardous material response
  • Search and rescue & disaster support

Operate under experienced company officers and established safety protocols.

🚑 EMS

Volunteer EMTs

Provide compassionate prehospital care when it matters most to patients and their families.

  • Emergency medical care and patient transport
  • Basic and advanced life support within scope
  • Multi-agency response and community medical standby

Certification & licensing support provided; ongoing skills training included.

🤝 Support

Support Members

Keep GFR mission-ready behind the scenes — every department needs strong support.

  • Assist with fundraising events & community outreach
  • Help with logistics, equipment, and station projects
  • Administrative and event-day coordination

Orientation and role-specific training provided. No prior experience needed.

What To Expect

Requirements & Training

Minimum Requirements
  • At least 16 years of age
  • Valid driver's license (for Fire/EMS positions)
  • No prior experience required — comprehensive training provided
Training & Commitment
  • Ongoing training ensures operational readiness and compliance with NFPA standards and state licensing requirements
  • Fire and EMS volunteers receive full training at no cost
  • Support members receive orientation and guidance tailored to their duties
  • Performance is evaluated for proficiency, effectiveness, and teamwork
